# THE ARD SOCIETY

# FOUNDING MEMBER AGREEMENT

*Kingdom of Ardtire Founding Initiative*

---

This Agreement is entered into between the Ard Society (represented by its Founder, Artur I) and the individual named below, upon their acceptance as a Founding Member of the Kingdom of Ardtire founding initiative. It is not a legal contract in the commercial sense — it is a statement of mutual understanding, commitment, and expectation between people who have chosen to undertake a serious project together.

Founding membership is the innermost level of participation in the Ard Society. It is reached through a progression of engagement — Forum Member, Community Member, and then Founding Member — and is extended by invitation only, after an extended period of mutual knowledge. This Agreement formalizes what both parties already understand from that process.

## **1. The Founding Member's Commitments**

### **1.1  Engagement**

The Founding Member commits to active and sustained engagement with the founding project — contributing their skills, knowledge, and judgment to the collective work of building the intellectual, organizational, and practical foundations of the Kingdom of Ardtire. Active engagement means regular participation commensurate with the Founding Member's circumstances, not a fixed number of hours — but it means genuine contribution, not passive observation.

### **1.2  Confidentiality**

The Founding Member agrees to treat as confidential the operational information shared at the Founding Member level — including but not limited to: territorial strategy and specific locations under consideration, financial and resource planning, the identities of other Founding Members who have not publicly identified themselves, and internal strategic discussions. This confidentiality obligation does not extend to the publicly available documents of the Ard Society, the governance philosophy, or the general nature of the project. The Founding Member may acknowledge their participation in the project to people they trust personally; they may not publicly disclose operational details.

### **1.3  Material Contribution**

As the founding effort progresses toward territory acquisition and physical establishment, Founding Members will be asked to make financial contributions toward the costs of founding, commensurate with their individual means. The amount, timing, and form of material contribution will be discussed individually and transparently — no Founding Member will be surprised by a financial request. All contributions will be accounted for with full transparency to the Founding Member group.

### **1.4  Good Faith**

The Founding Member agrees to engage in all aspects of the project in good faith — honestly, constructively, and with genuine commitment to the shared goal. Disagreement and dissent are expected and welcome; bad faith, deception, or conduct that materially harms the project or its members are not.

## **2. The Society's Commitments**

### **2.1  Transparency**

The Ard Society commits to full transparency with Founding Members about the project's strategic direction, financial position, territorial thinking, and the reasoning behind significant decisions. Founding Members are not passive recipients of a vision — they are participants in its development and are entitled to the information necessary to contribute meaningfully.

### **2.2  Recognition**

Founding Members will be recognized in the historical record of the Kingdom of Ardtire's establishment. The founding generation — the people who did the work before there was anything to show for it — will not be forgotten. The specific form of recognition, including any privileges or honors that attach to founding status, will be developed in the constitutional drafting process.

### **2.3  Good Faith**

The Ard Society and its Founder commit to engaging with every Founding Member in good faith — honestly, with respect for their contribution, and with genuine consideration for their interests and concerns. The power differential inherent in a founding project does not excuse the Founder from the obligations of fair dealing toward those who have placed their trust in the project.

## **3. Withdrawal**

A Founding Member may withdraw from the project at any time by notifying the Founder in writing. Withdrawal does not nullify the confidentiality obligation in clause 1.2, which persists for a period of five years from the date of withdrawal with respect to operational information received during Founding Member status.

Material contributions made prior to withdrawal are not refundable as a general rule — they represent contributions to a founding effort, not an investment in a commercial enterprise. In cases where a Founding Member withdraws due to circumstances beyond their control, or where the project materially changes from what was represented at the time of the contribution, the Founder commits to considering requests for partial return of contributions in good faith.

Founding membership may be revoked by the Founder for material breach of this Agreement — specifically for breach of confidentiality, bad faith conduct that harms the project or its members, or conduct inconsistent with the values of the founding community. Revocation will not be exercised arbitrarily; the grounds will be documented and the affected member will have the opportunity to respond before a final decision is made.

## **4. The Nature of This Agreement**

This Agreement is a statement of mutual commitment between people who have chosen to undertake an extraordinary project together. It is not a commercial contract and is not intended to be enforceable in the courts of any existing jurisdiction — not because its terms are unserious, but because the project it concerns is not yet a legal entity in those jurisdictions, and because the obligations it describes are fundamentally matters of honor and trust rather than litigation.

When the Kingdom of Ardtire is formally established and its legal institutions are operational, the terms of this Agreement — and the recognition of founding membership — will be incorporated into the kingdom's own legal framework as that framework develops. Until then, it stands on the integrity of the people who sign it.
