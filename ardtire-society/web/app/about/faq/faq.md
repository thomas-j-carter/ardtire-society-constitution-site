# **Frequently Asked Questions**

*Honest answers to the questions we expect, including the difficult ones*

These are the questions that serious readers ask. We have tried to answer them with the same directness that characterizes the rest of this site. If your question is not here, the Apply page has a contact option — we read everything.

## THE BASICS
---

### **Is this serious?**

Yes. The Kingdom of Ardtire is a genuine founding effort — not a thought experiment, not a game, not a community organized around a fantasy. The governance documents on this site represent real constitutional thinking developed over years. The founder has made a real commitment of time and resources. The community being built here is intended to produce a functioning sovereign state. We understand that this is an extraordinary claim. We also understand that the only way to demonstrate seriousness is through sustained action over time, which is what we are doing.

### **Is this legal?**

The activities of the Ard Society — discussing political philosophy, developing governance models, building a community of like-minded people, publishing ideas — are entirely lawful in every democratic jurisdiction. Freedom of thought, expression, and association protect them. The Society is not engaged in any illegal activity, does not advocate for violence or unlawful means, and conducts itself with full awareness of the legal environments in which its members live. The question of how the Kingdom of Ardtire formally comes into being — the pathway to recognized sovereignty — is a legal and political question that we are approaching with professional counsel and with a long-term strategy. We are not asking anyone to break any law.

### **Is this a scam?**

We are glad you asked this directly because it deserves a direct answer. No. The Ard Society does not ask for money from Forum Members or Community Members. There is no investment opportunity, no token sale, no subscription fee, no product being sold. The project asks for your time and your serious engagement, and in later stages — for Founding Members who have been vetted over an extended period — it will ask for financial contribution commensurate with your means toward the actual costs of founding. That ask will be made openly, to people who know exactly what they are contributing to and why, with full transparency about how resources are used.

> If anyone contacts you claiming to represent the Ard Society and asks for money before you have reached Founding Member status through the processes described on this site, do not pay them. Contact us immediately. That is not us.

### **Has anything like this ever succeeded?**

New sovereignty projects have a mixed historical record, and we will not pretend otherwise. Most fail — through lack of resources, lack of the right people, legal and political obstacles that prove insurmountable, or the internal fractures that affect any community under sustained pressure. The ones that have succeeded or made substantial progress — Liberland, the Free State Project as a community-building model, various charter city initiatives — share common features: serious foundational work, a core group of highly capable committed people, a realistic strategy for the territory question, and the patience to play a long game. We are attempting to build all of those features deliberately rather than hoping they emerge. We are also building on a richer intellectual foundation and a more coherent governance model than most comparable efforts. That does not guarantee success. It improves the probability.

## THE GOVERNANCE MODEL
---

### **Why a monarchy? Isn't that backwards?**

This question is worth taking seriously rather than dismissing. The short answer is: because the structural arguments for hereditary executive monarchy are stronger than the democratic consensus has allowed them to be, and because the structural failures of mass electoral democracy are more fundamental than reformers have been willing to acknowledge. The longer answer is in the Philosophy page. The governance model we are building is not absolute monarchy — it is a semi-constitutional system with inviolable individual rights, an independent judiciary, and a deliberative assembly with real power. The case for it rests on the time horizon argument, the stewardship principle, and the observable failure of electoral incentive structures over generational timescales. We invite you to engage with those arguments on their merits.

### **What prevents the monarch from becoming a tyrant?**

This is the right question and the one the governance model was designed to answer. Four structural constraints operate simultaneously. First, the fundamental law establishes four inviolable rights that no power within the kingdom — including the sovereign — can suspend or remove: freedom of conscience, security of person, security of property, and freedom of expression. Second, an independent judiciary with a Supreme Constitutional Court empowered to strike down royal decrees that violate the fundamental law. Third, a deliberative assembly that holds the power of financial appropriation — the sovereign cannot fund governance without the Chamber of Citizens' consent, which is the most powerful practical check on executive authority in any constitutional system. Fourth, the dynastic interest itself: a monarch whose children inherit the realm has a structural incentive to govern it well that no electoral politician possesses. None of these guarantees perfection. Together they constitute a serious architecture against the specific failure modes of unchecked power.

### **How is the founder accountable before the kingdom exists?**

In this phase, the founder is accountable to the founding community in the most direct way: through transparency about the project's direction and decisions, through the trust that is built or eroded by how commitments are kept over time, and through the fact that the founding effort only succeeds if capable people continue to choose to participate in it. A founder who behaves badly in this phase will not attract or retain the people the project needs. That is a real and immediate accountability, distinct from the constitutional accountability that the governance model will establish once the kingdom exists. We also note that the founder has committed to operating under the same constitutional constraints as any future monarch — this is not a project designed to create unchecked power for its originator.

## PARTICIPATION
---

### **Do I have to move there eventually?**

Not immediately, and not as a condition of participation in the current phase. The community is online, and engagement at the Forum Member and Community Member levels requires no change to your current life or location. Founding membership, in time, will involve a genuine expectation of physical presence and relocation — you cannot help build a physical society from a permanent distance. But that stage is some way off, and the conversation between the project and any individual founding member about what their specific contribution and commitment looks like will be honest and individualized. Nobody will be asked to uproot their life without full knowledge of what they are being asked to do and why.

### **What happens to my current citizenship?**

Nothing, at this stage. Participating in the Ard Society — discussing political philosophy, engaging in an online community — has no effect whatsoever on your current citizenship or legal status. When the kingdom is formally established and citizenship is formally offered, individuals will face the question that every person who has ever emigrated or naturalized has faced: what citizenship do you hold, and what are the implications of holding or acquiring another? Many nations permit dual citizenship; others do not. The legal landscape around citizenship in a new state is complex, involves the laws of multiple jurisdictions, and requires professional legal advice specific to each individual's situation. We will not offer generalized guidance on this question because generalized guidance on legal matters of this kind is not responsible. What we can say is that it will be a question each person navigates on their own terms, with full information, at a time that is a long way from now.

### **What skills are most needed right now?**

In the current phase, the most valuable contribution is intellectual engagement — the capacity to think seriously about governance, philosophy, and the practical challenges of founding, and to do so in writing that advances the collective understanding of the community. Beyond that, the skills of highest long-term priority are: law (constitutional, international, property, and maritime), medicine, civil and structural engineering, agriculture and food systems, finance and accounting, information security, military and security disciplines, and the skilled trades. We want to be clear that these skill categories are not gatekeeping criteria for Forum membership — the first bar is genuine intellectual engagement, and specialists in the above domains who cannot think carefully about ideas are less valuable than thoughtful generalists. But if you have expertise in any of these areas and the intellectual qualities we are looking for, you are precisely the kind of person this project needs.

### **How long will this take?**

Longer than optimists hope and shorter than pessimists predict, which is an honest answer even if it is not a satisfying one. Assembling a founding group of the right people, developing the resources necessary to act, identifying and acquiring territory, and navigating the political and legal pathway to recognized sovereignty is work measured in years and probably in decades for its fullest expression. Anyone who tells you a new state can be established quickly is either mistaken or misleading you. We are building for permanence, which means building at the pace that permanence requires. The question to ask yourself is not whether this will be fast — it will not — but whether it is worth doing at the pace it requires. We believe it is.

## THE TERRITORY
---

### **Where will Ardtire be?**

We are not publishing the territorial strategy at this stage, and we will explain why honestly: the territory question is the most operationally sensitive aspect of the project, and naming specific locations or jurisdictions before the founding group is assembled and the resources to act are in place would be strategically counterproductive. What we can say is that the character of the territory we are seeking has been described in the Project documents — maritime access, substantial scale, a moderate climate — and that the strategy for how we get there is being developed with legal and political seriousness. Territory will be discussed in full with founding members as they reach that stage of participation.

### **How do you get sovereignty over land that belongs to another state?**

This is the central political and legal challenge of any new sovereignty project, and there is no simple answer. The historical pathways include negotiated autonomy arrangements with host states, the charter city or special economic zone framework in which a host state grants substantial self-governance rights in exchange for investment and development, the long-term development of a community that becomes a political reality a host state eventually recognizes, and in rare cases formal declarations of independence in circumstances that make them politically viable. None of these pathways is fast, certain, or without obstacles. The appropriate pathway for Ardtire will be determined by the territory ultimately identified and the political circumstances that pertain there. This is work for lawyers, diplomats, and political strategists as much as for founding visionaries — and we are building the team accordingly.

## IDENTITY AND CULTURE
---

### **Do I have to be of Irish or Scottish descent to join?**

No. The Gaelic heritage of the Ardtire name is a cultural and philosophical touchstone, not an ethnic requirement. The kingdom's name draws on a tradition of language, governance, and identity that we find meaningful and worth carrying forward — but the community we are building is open to anyone who shares the founding values, regardless of ancestry, nationality, or background. What matters is alignment with the philosophy, the character to contribute constructively, and the commitment to build something worth building. We have no ethnic criteria and no intention of developing any.

### **What is the role of religion in Ardtire?**

The Kingdom of Ardtire has no established religion. Freedom of conscience — the right to hold, practice, or decline any religious belief without interference or penalty from the state — is one of the four inviolable rights in the constitutional framework, which means it cannot be removed or qualified by any power within the kingdom under any circumstances. People of faith and people without faith are equally welcome in this community and will be equally protected as citizens. The kingdom's character will inevitably be shaped over time by the beliefs and practices of the people who build it, but that is a matter for the community to develop organically — not for the founding documents to prescribe.
