# Terms of Community

*The standards of conduct that govern life in the Ard Society community*

A community is not only defined by what it welcomes — it is defined by what it will not tolerate. The terms below are not a long list of prohibitions; they are an expression of the values that make substantive community possible. Read them in that spirit.

These terms apply to all members of the Ard Society community at every level of participation — Forum Members, Community Members, and Founding Members alike. No level of seniority exempts a member from the standards described here.

* **I. The Standard We Hold** *

The Ard Society is a community of serious people engaged in a serious project. The standard of conduct we expect reflects that. We are not a social media platform optimized for engagement; we are a community organized around the long and difficult work of founding. The culture we are building here is the culture that will, in time, characterize the Kingdom of Ardtire itself.

> The animating principle is this: engage as you would wish to be engaged with. Argue as you would wish to be argued with. Disagree as a person who respects the person they are disagreeing with. We are building something together. Conduct yourself accordingly.

* **II. What Is Expected** *

**Intellectual Honesty**

Say what you believe. Acknowledge when you are wrong. Update your views when the evidence or argument warrants it. The community is built on the assumption that its members are genuinely trying to think well rather than simply to win arguments. Members who argue in bad faith — who misrepresent positions, manufacture evidence, or engage in deliberate obfuscation — will be removed.

**Substantive Engagement**

Engage with what is actually being said rather than with a caricature of it. If you disagree with a position, say why — with argument, not assertion. Short dismissals, contemptuous one-liners, and refusals to engage with the substance of what others write are not contributions. They will be treated as failures of participation rather than acts of it.

**Respect for Persons**

Critique ideas, not people. The distinction between a sharp challenge to an argument and an attack on the person making it is real and is expected to be observed. Disagreement — even vigorous disagreement — is entirely welcome. Personal hostility, contempt, mockery, and harassment are not.

**Constructive Orientation**
This community exists to build something. Members who consistently contribute only negativity, grievance, or destructive criticism without constructive purpose are failing the community's purpose regardless of whether their specific complaints are valid. We expect members to bring energy that helps the project move forward, not energy that impedes it.

* **III. What Is Not Permitted** *
The following conduct will result in removal from the community without extended process. These are not judgment calls — they are bright lines.

- **Harassment**  —  sustained hostile conduct directed at any member, including in private channels.
- **Advocacy of violence**  —  calling for, celebrating, or facilitating violence against any person or group.
- **Ethnic or racial contempt**  —  expression of hostility toward any person or group on the basis of ethnicity, race, national origin, or religion.
- **Deliberate deception**  —  intentional misrepresentation of identity, qualifications, or facts material to the community or the project.
- **Breach of confidentiality**  —  disclosure of information shared in confidence at the Community Member or Founding Member level.
- **Conduct harmful to the project**  —  deliberate action intended to damage the reputation, legal standing, or operational capacity of the Ard Society or the Kingdom of Ardtire founding effort.

* **IV. Moderation** *
The community is moderated by the Founder and by Community Members and Founding Members who have been designated as moderators. Moderation decisions are made in good faith with reference to these Terms. Members who believe a moderation decision was made in error may request a review by contacting the Founder directly.

Moderation actions range from a private note raising a concern, through a formal warning posted to the relevant thread, through temporary suspension of posting privileges, to permanent removal from the community. The severity of the action will be proportionate to the conduct. Bright-line violations described in Section III will result in immediate removal without prior warning.

Moderators are not infallible and are not above the Terms they enforce. A moderator who applies these Terms capriciously, in bad faith, or in ways that serve personal agendas rather than community standards will be removed from the moderator role. The integrity of the moderation function is itself a community value.

* **V. Evolution of These Terms** *

These Terms will develop as the community develops. Significant changes will be communicated to all members before they take effect, with an opportunity for community input. The core principles — intellectual honesty, respect for persons, constructive orientation, and the bright-line prohibitions — are not subject to revision. The specific application of those principles in particular circumstances may evolve with the community's experience.

By participating in the Ard Society community at any level, members accept these Terms and the authority of the moderation structure they describe. Members who find these Terms fundamentally incompatible with how they wish to engage are invited to reflect on whether this is the right community for them.
